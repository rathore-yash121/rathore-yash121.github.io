Mixed Reality Human-Robot Collaboration

Mixed Reality Human-Robot Collaboration

Feb 2016 - still working

Interacting with robots in a collaborative environment often turns out difficult especially when both the humans and the robot desires to work simultaneously on a set of tasks. Hence to overcome this barrier, we are working on a research project based on a mixed-reality communication system, for an intuitive human-robot collaboration. The main objective of this approach is to utilize the physical work environment as a canvas to communicate task-related instructions and robot intentions in the form of visual cues. The system can guide the human while he/she is performing tasks, warn them by clearly displaying the intentions of the robot, thereby creating a more safer working environment. The user can in-turn improve their precision and efficiency.

Team: Yash Rathore, Ramsundar K G, Heni Ben Amor

Blind Dating: Making Mobile Dating Accessible for People with Visual Impairments

Blind Dating: Making Mobile Dating Accessible for People with Visual Impairments

Feb 2017 - April 2017

Online dating platforms such as Tinder, Bumble, and Coffee Meets Bagel, have become culturally accepted methods of meeting new people. However, many of these online dating platform’s user profiles rely heavily on visual data. For individuals with visual impairments, the way that these applications are designed presents a barrier to the opportunity for them to form new relationships. Using voice-over with any of these apps creates profound confusion as they are not built with accessibility in mind. We proposed a method of providing users with visual impairments access to the data encoded in the images of other user’s profiles.

To find what information people care about knowing the most in a dating profile, we surveyed the public and then built simple classifiers for the most popular features that were identified by the survey. As the APIs for the dating apps mentioned above are not public, we created an application that works via screenshots. When the user would like to know more about a given profile, they just need to activate the native screenshot functionality, and our application will find the screenshot in the screenshot folder on the phone, send it to our web service where the image is analyzed and output text is formed. This text is sent back to the phone and read out, providing the user with a coarse idea of what the image contained.

Team: Corey Heath, Meredith Moore, Yash Rathore, Anusha Rajan, Guru V, Pawan O

Disease Detection and Classification in Soya Plants

Disease Detection and Classification in Soya Plants.

Aug 2013 - Nov 2013

Monitoring crops for diseases detection require a lot of human expertise. However this might be prohibitively expensive in large farms and even may not be available in remote places. To overcome this issue and increase the productivity of Soya bean farms we developed a computer vision based mechanism to monitor plants for leaf diseases. The images of infected leaves are processed to obtain textural and color features and are fed to a classifier to detect the disease type. The machine classifier is trained with large number of labeled training samples.

- The main objective of this project was to detect and then classify the various diseases in Soya bean plant using Computer Vision and Soft Computing techniques where the input is in the form of visual data.

- Secondly, we tried using different methods for classification and choose the most efficient one based on the compared results.
